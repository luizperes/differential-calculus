We know that quadratic functions can be describes as $y = a(x + h)^2 + k$, thus:
$y = a(x - 6)^2 + k = 0$ when $x = 6 \therefore k = 0$.
Now, we just need to substitute $y$ and $x$ with the point given $(7,2)$:\\
$f(x) = y$\\
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
    y & = a(x - 6)^2 + 0\\
    2 & = a(7 - 6)^2\\
    a & = 2\\
    & \therefore\\
    f(x) & = 2(x - 6)^2
\end{split}
\end{equation}
